CARB Frequently Asked Questions

The California Air Resources Board (CARB) is a state agency under the California Environmental Protection Agency responsible for regulating air quality and vehicle emissions in California.

CARB matters for your truck because:

  • California has strict emissions standards for commercial vehicles
  • Non-compliant trucks may face fines and operational restrictions
  • CARB regulations affect truck registration and operation within California
  • Compliance is required for most commercial trucks operating in the state

The Advanced Clean Trucks (ACT) regulation, adopted by CARB in June 2020, requires manufacturers to transition to zero-emission trucks.

Key points:

  • Manufacturers must sell an increasing percentage of zero-emission trucks starting in 2024
  • Sales requirements range from 5-75% depending on truck class by 2035
  • Applies to medium and heavy-duty trucks (Class 2b-8)
  • Large entities must report their annual truck purchases and fleet information

Read our detailed ACT regulation guide

The Truck and Bus Regulation requires diesel trucks and buses that operate in California to be upgraded to reduce emissions. Nearly all diesel-fueled trucks and buses with a gross vehicle weight rating (GVWR) greater than 14,000 pounds are subject to this regulation.

Requirements include:

  • Older engines must be replaced with newer, cleaner engines (2010 model year or newer)
  • Diesel particulate filters (DPF) must be installed on certain older engines
  • Vehicles must meet specific emission standards based on model year
  • Compliance deadlines vary by vehicle type and usage

Operating a non-compliant truck in California can result in significant penalties:

  • Fines ranging from $300 to $1,000+ per vehicle per day
  • Vehicle registration may be blocked or suspended
  • Fleet owners may face additional penalties
  • Potential impoundment of non-compliant vehicles
  • Loss of California operating privileges

The Hybrid and Zero-Emission Truck and Bus Voucher Incentive Project (HVIP) provides point-of-sale rebates to help California fleets purchase or lease zero-emission and hybrid vehicles.

Benefits include:

  • Vouchers up to $400,000 for zero-emission trucks
  • Reduced upfront costs for clean vehicle purchases
  • Simplified application process
  • First-come, first-served funding availability
  • Helps offset the cost of CARB compliance upgrades

Whether you need a diesel particulate filter (DPF) depends on your truck's model year and engine configuration:

  • Trucks with 2010+ engines typically have DPFs installed from the factory
  • Older trucks (2007-2009) may need aftermarket DPF installation
  • Pre-2007 trucks must either install a DPF or upgrade to a newer engine
  • DPFs must be CARB-verified and properly maintained
  • Regular DPF cleaning and maintenance is required

Great question! When you complete your CARB inspection before your scheduled due date, your next inspection date is still calculated from your original due date, not from the date you tested early.

Here's an example:

  • Your original due date: June 1, 2024
  • You test early on: March 1, 2024 (90 days before due date)
  • Your next test will be due: December 1, 2024 (6 months from June 1st)
  • NOT September 1, 2024 (which would be 6 months from March 1st)

Key benefit: Testing early gives you peace of mind and ensures compliance without penalizing you by shortening your inspection cycle. You maintain your regular inspection schedule while staying ahead of deadlines.
